Contracts – Module 1: Lesson

CONTRACTS – MODULE 1: INTRO TO CONTRACT FORMATION

Lesson: Offer, Acceptance, and Consideration

A contract is a legally enforceable agreement. To be valid, it must include:
1. Offer: A clear proposal by one party (offeror) to another (offeree).
2. Acceptance: An unequivocal agreement to the terms of the offer.
3. Consideration: Something of value exchanged by both parties.

Example: If Alex offers to sell a bike to Jordan for $100, and Jordan says yes, the agreement is valid if both provide consideration (the bike and the money).

Flashcards

1. Q: What are the three elements of a valid contract?
   A: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration

2. Q: What is an "offer" in contract law?
   A: A proposal that, if accepted, creates a binding agreement.

3. Q: What is "acceptance"?
   A: An unambiguous agreement to the terms of the offer.

4. Q: What is "consideration"?
   A: Value exchanged by both parties in a contract.

5. Q: Can a promise to do something you’re already obligated to do be consideration?
   A: No.

6. Q: What case established unilateral contract theory?
   A: Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co.

7. Q: Can silence be acceptance?
   A: Usually no, unless prior dealings or implied consent exist.

8. Q: What is a counteroffer?
   A: A rejection of the original offer and a new offer in its place.

Quiz

   a) Offer

   d) Justice

2. What happens if a party accepts an offer with changes?
   Answer: It becomes a counteroffer.

   a) Fair
   b) Valuable

   Answer: b

4. True or False: Silence can never be considered acceptance.
   Answer: False

   Answer: Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co.

Case Brief

CASE BRIEF – CARLILL V. CARBOLIC SMOKE BALL CO. (1893)

Facts: Carlill used a smoke ball product advertised to prevent flu. The company promised £100 to anyone who used it and still got sick. She did. They refused to pay.

Issue: Was the advertisement a binding offer?

Holding: Yes.

Reasoning: The ad constituted a unilateral offer, accepted through performance.

Rule: A unilateral contract is formed when one party promises something in exchange for performance.

Conclusion: Carlill won and was entitled to £100.

Glossary Terms

Offer – A proposal to form a contract.
Acceptance – An agreement to the offer’s terms.
Consideration – Something of value exchanged.
Counteroffer – A response that changes the terms of the original offer.
Unilateral Contract – A contract accepted by performing an act.
