Lesson: Offer, Acceptance, and Consideration A contract is a legally enforceable agreement. To be valid, it must include: 1. Offer – A clear proposal to make a deal. 2. Acceptance – A clear agreement to that deal. 3. Consideration – Something of value exchanged by both parties. Example: If Alex offers to sell a bike for $100 and Jordan agrees, a valid contract is formed.
QUIZ: 1. Which is NOT an element of a contract? a) Offer b) Acceptance c) Consideration d) Justice Answer: d 2. What happens if a party accepts with different terms? Answer: It's a counteroffer. 3. True or False: Silence is always acceptance. Answer: False
FLASHCARDS: - Q: What are the 3 elements of a contract? A: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration - Q: What is a counteroffer? A: A rejection of the original offer with new terms
CASE BRIEF – Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co. Facts: A woman used a smoke ball as directed but still caught flu. She sued for the £100 reward. Issue: Was the ad a binding contract? Held: Yes. The ad was a unilateral offer accepted through use.
GLOSSARY: - Offer: A proposal to enter into a contract. - Acceptance: Agreement to the terms of the offer. - Consideration: Something of value exchanged in the contract.
