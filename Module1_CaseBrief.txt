CASE BRIEF – CARLILL V. CARBOLIC SMOKE BALL CO. (1893)

Facts: Carlill used a smoke ball product advertised to prevent flu. The company promised £100 to anyone who used it and still got sick. She did. They refused to pay.

Issue: Was the advertisement a binding offer?

Holding: Yes.

Reasoning: The ad constituted a unilateral offer, accepted through performance.

Rule: A unilateral contract is formed when one party promises something in exchange for performance.

Conclusion: Carlill won and was entitled to £100.
